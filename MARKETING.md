# Jackdaw: moat & killer features

The single source of truth for **how we position and sell Jackdaw**. Written so any agent (copy, web,
social, sales) can read it and produce on-message work. Keep it tight; update it as the product moves.

---

## The one-liner
**Jackdaw is the songwriter's DAW, the smallest of the crows and the smartest.** It does only what it
takes to turn an idea into a finished-enough, shareable song, beautifully. Nothing else.

## The thesis (our wedge)
**Every other DAW wants to do everything.** They bury the song under a thousand menus, faders and consoles
built for mixing and mastering. Jackdaw is **a means to an end: more art.** We are unapologetically *not* a
mixing/mastering tool. Finishing goes to a real DAW via Export. We own the part where songs are *written,
versioned, and shared*. Small on purpose, like the bird.

## Who it's for (and who it isn't)
- **The writer** (think Taylor): records ideas, writes lyrics/chords/tabs, keeps references, a full writing
  workflow without leaving the app.
- **The producer** (think Jack): gets the robust bits that matter for writing (Sidetrack, folder/submix
  groups, plugins, automation) without the bloat.
- **The collaborating pair:** a writer + producer, or writer + mixer, passing a song back and forth.
- **NOT** mix engineers or mastering engineers. We don't compete for "finishing the sound." Saying no here
  is the strategy, not a gap.

## Voice and tone

**Register.** Primary is dry and confident: short, plain, sure of itself. Warm up in longer body copy so it
sounds like a musician, not a brochure. One cheeky line per page, never more. Save the lyrical register for
big emotional beats (the hero, the closer). Lean on the jackdaw as seasoning: smallest crow, cleverest,
bonded for life, builds a nest, keeps every shiny idea.

**Who is talking.** A person, not a company. A musician who built the tool they wanted and is telling you
about it. First person is good ("I built this," "we're a couple of people and a bird"). Sign things. A real
human wrote this and it should read like it, not like a committee approved it.

**The feeling we're after.** "Big companies want your money. This was made by an artist who wants you to make
more art." Show it, do not chant it. Our values live in our choices: no cloud, no accounts, nothing
harvested, your files stay on your machine, solo is free and stays free. We charge for collaboration so we
can keep eating, and we say that plainly instead of hiding it. No dark patterns, no upsell nags, no "unlock"
guilt. Name the enemy gently (bloated everything-DAWs, subscription traps, tools that want your money more
than your music), but never preach and never sound superior.

**On the verge of weird.** Be specific and a little odd. Weird comes from real detail (the riff you hummed
in the kitchen at 2am), not random quirk. Admit limits to people's faces ("Jackdaw can't master your track.
On purpose. Go use a real mastering tool."). Honesty is disarming, and almost nobody else does it.

**Rules.**
- No em-dashes. Ever. Use a period, a comma, parentheses, or a line break. A short sentence does the job a
  dash was reaching for.
- Cut every word you can. Remove it, did the meaning survive, then it was never needed.
- Second person, present tense, active voice.
- Speak musician, not engineer. Riff, take, chorus. Not signal chain, gain staging.
- Confidence through subtraction, never hype.
- The bird is seasoning, not a theme park. One metaphor per page.
- One joke per page, not ten.

**Never say.** Seamless, powerful, robust (in copy), revolutionary, game-changing, empowering, leverage,
solutions, passionate, "excited to announce," "unlock your potential," "elevate your sound," "for creators."
If it could appear in any startup launch tweet, delete it.

## The moat: killer features
Each: what it is · why only us · the hook.

### 1. Stripped-down, on purpose
- **What:** the basics done exquisitely (record, arrange, simple mix) with the producer essentials
  (Sidetrack, folder/submix groups, plugins, automation) and the writer essentials, and nothing else.
- **Why only us:** everyone else adds; we subtract. Simplicity is the feature, and it's the hardest to copy
  because it's a *decision*, not a capability.
- **Hook:** "Write the song. Skip the spaceship."

### 2. Collaboration and open sessions (no cloud, no middleman)
- **What:** send an individual **track**, a whole **session**, or just **notes** straight to a collaborator,
  peer-to-peer and **encrypted** (over Croc). No accounts, no upload, no link.
- **Naming:** you **send a Track Egg / Session Egg**; they **hatch** it and it lands in their project.
- **Open sessions (a flagship use case):** make a session public and drop the code anywhere (Reddit, Discord,
  a group chat, a tweet). Anyone hatches it and gets the whole song to remix, finish, or learn from. This is
  also the growth engine: every code posted is one more person installing Jackdaw to open it. We never gate
  this, on purpose.
- **Why only us:** web DAWs are built for collab but too limited for serious producers; desktop DAWs have no
  real sharing at all. Nobody does it this openly or this well.
- **Killer advantage, the plugin-compatibility problem nobody solved:** when you send a track we include the
  **plugin identity + presets**; if the recipient owns the plugins, they get the live, editable chain; if
  not, they get the **bounced audio** automatically. They never get a broken session again.
- **Hook:** "Stop emailing files. Send it beak to beak."
- **Hook (open):** "Post the code. Let the whole internet hatch your song."

### 3. Ideas Library: not every idea is a song
- **What:** right-click any clip and save it to an organized **ideas rack**; it carries its **BPM** (and
  key/source). Browse your catalog and drop a saved riff into any song later.
- **Why only us:** it's "build your own Splice, out of your own ideas." No DAW turns throwaway takes into a
  reusable, tempo-aware personal library.
- **Hook:** "Millions of ideas die because they didn't fit *that* song. Keep them for the next one."

### 4. Auto-MP3 + the companion app (listen anywhere, capture anywhere)
- **What:** every save **auto-renders an MP3**, so a song is always playable without opening the DAW. A
  simple **companion app** (Mac/PC/Android/iOS) lets you listen to your demos, one by one or in **radio
  mode** ("Jackdaw FM"), and **record voice notes that sync back into the DAW with a short code** (via Croc).
- **Why only us:** your unfinished songs become a private radio station you carry everywhere; ideas captured
  on a walk land back in the session. No DAW has a listening/capture companion.
- **Hook:** "A radio of your own unfinished songs. In your pocket."

### 5. All-in-one writing surface
- **What:** lyrics, chords, **tabs**, markdown notes, images, video and **YouTube/streaming references**
  live inside the song. Comments can be **text or a voice note pinned to the exact bar**.
- **Why only us:** the whole writing workflow in one place. No more Google Docs for lyrics, no more "at 1:32
  I thought about…". Hum the riff into a voice note instead of describing it.
- **Hook:** "Bring the whole nest. Everything you used to alt-tab for, in the DAW."

### 6. Focus mode
- **What:** a minimalist, full-screen, songwriting-first experience. Hit record, capture one take, then
  **trim the sections you love straight into your Ideas Library**.
- **Why only us:** no DAW offers a distraction-free writing room. The internet can wait.
- **Hook:** "A nest the world can't reach."

### 7. Revisions: every version of the song, in one project
- **What:** immutable **snapshots** of a song you can jump between and share. Explore a different direction
  without fear; the version you liked is always one click back. Linear, collaborative history (Taylor's
  guitar+voice V1 → Jack's V2 → …).
- **Why only us:** regular DAWs force you to "Save As" a whole new project, leaving ghost copies nobody
  deletes. We make versioning a first-class, shareable button.
- **Hook:** "No more Song_final_FINAL_v2. One project, every version."

### 8. Yours to look at: deep customization
- **What:** a token-based UI means real themes, track sizing, layout and shortcuts. Shape the room to your
  taste and mood.
- **Why only us:** because the UI is web tech on design tokens, we re-skin in ways native DAWs can't easily
  match.
- **Hook:** "Every jackdaw has its own shimmer."

## Messaging pillars (use these as headlines)
1. **Small on purpose.** The anti-everything DAW, a means to more art.
2. **Beak to beak.** Collaboration with no cloud, and the plugin problem finally solved.
3. **Never lose an idea.** Ideas Library, Revisions, and a radio of your own demos.
4. **The whole song in one place.** Lyrics, chords, tabs, references, voice notes.
5. **A room of your own.** Focus mode and deep customization.
6. **Share a song with the world.** Post a session code anywhere, anyone hatches it (and discovers Jackdaw).
7. **Work with your ears, not your eyes.** Meters stay hidden by default. You listen first and look only when it matters: the track you're on, a clip, or the mixer when you open it.

## Naming decisions
- **Sharing:** Track Egg / Session Egg → recipient **hatches** it. (Ties to the nest/egg brand.)
- **Radio / companion listening:** **Jackdaw FM.**
- **Ideas rack:** **Ideas Library.**
- **Versions:** **Revisions** (locked, this is the public name). Internally the spec calls these "Takes";
  align the product to **Revisions** so there's one term everywhere.

## Copy library (on-voice examples)
Drop-in copy in the final voice. No em-dashes, written by a person, art over money. Use as-is or as a tuning
fork.

**Hero / one-liner.** "A small DAW for writing songs. Made by someone who'd rather be writing songs."

**1. Stripped-down on purpose.** "Most DAWs hand you a cockpit and dare you to fly it. We took almost
everything out. What's left is the song. (Yes, on purpose. No, we're not adding a spectral analyzer.)"

**2. Collaboration / eggs.** "Tuck a track into an egg. Send it to anyone. They hatch it and it opens in
their session, plugins and presets and all. No cloud touched it. No account asked. And if they don't own
your plugins, they get a clean bounce instead, so nothing ever lands broken. We spent a long time on this
because it wrecked our week more than once."

**2b. Open sessions.** "Made something you want the world to mess with? Make the session public, drop the
code on Reddit or in your group chat, and anyone can hatch the whole song. Remix it, finish it, take it
apart to see how it works. Every code you post is one more person who just found Jackdaw, which is pretty
much the only marketing we've got."

**3. Ideas Library.** "Not every idea is a song. Some are just a good four bars you hummed in the kitchen.
Right-click, keep it. It remembers its tempo. Build a shelf of your own riffs and steal from yourself later.
Past you had taste."

**4. Auto-mp3 + companion (coming soon).** "Every save quietly bounces an mp3, so your songs play back
without opening anything. Soon there's a little app for your phone that plays them too, and shuffles your
demos like a radio station only you can hear. Hum a melody into it on a walk and it lands back in your
session. We're building it now."

**5. All-in-one writing surface.** "Lyrics, chords, tabs, notes, and the YouTube video you keep saying 'make
it feel like this' about. All inside the song, not across seven tabs. Leave a comment as a voice note pinned
to the exact bar. Hum the part instead of typing 'you know, the bit that goes da-da-DUM.'"

**6. Focus mode.** "A full screen. Dark. A record button. That's the whole thing. The internet can wait
twenty minutes. Capture one take, keep the parts you love, the rest slips quietly onto your ideas shelf.
Nobody's watching. Make something embarrassing."

**7. Revisions.** "You know the graveyard. Song_final. Song_final_v2. Song_ACTUAL_final. Gone. Every version
lives in one place. Jump back to the one you liked. Send v3 to your co-writer, get v4 back. Nothing you make
disappears."

**8. Customization.** "Make it yours. Actually yours. Themes, sizes, shortcuts, the whole room. We built the
look on the same stuff websites are made of, so you can bend it further than any beige factory DAW will let
you."

**Closer / who we are.** "We're not a big company. No growth team, no plan to sell your data (we don't have
your data, it lives on your machine). Solo Jackdaw is free and it stays free. We charge for collaboration so
we can keep the lights on, and we'll always say that to your face. We made this because the world needs more
songs in it, and the tools kept getting in the way. That's the whole pitch. Go make something."

## Honesty note (don't market vaporware as shipped)
Mark these **"coming soon"** in public copy until they actually ship. They're roadmap, not current: the
**companion app + voice-note sync**, **Jackdaw FM / auto-MP3 playback**, the **Ideas Library**, the
**Focus-mode capture-to-ideas** flow, and the **ears-first meters** (metering isn't built yet). Shipped-or-near (record/arrange, Sidetrack, folders, plugins, themes,
P2P track sharing, Revisions) can be marketed as real. Verify status before a launch campaign.
